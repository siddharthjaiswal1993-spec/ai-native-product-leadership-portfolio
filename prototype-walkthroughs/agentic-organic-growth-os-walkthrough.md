# Agentic Organic Growth OS — Walkthrough Guide

**GitHub:** https://github.com/siddharthjaiswal1993-spec/agentic-organic-growth-os

---

## Demo Goal

Show how a coordinated agent platform for content strategy, SEO/GEO visibility, and brand governance can operate across content planning, publishing, and performance monitoring — with human approval at each stage.

---

## What Problem This Demonstrates

Organic growth for B2B SaaS is labor-intensive and poorly coordinated: content strategy is disconnected from SEO data, brand voice is inconsistent across writers and channels, and performance feedback loops are too slow to inform content decisions in real time. This project explores what a multi-agent organic growth platform looks like when the agents coordinate across content planning, production, distribution, and measurement.

---

## Primary Persona

**Head of Content / VP Marketing at a 100-person B2B SaaS company.** Owns organic growth: SEO, content production, brand voice, and distribution. Current process: monthly content planning in a spreadsheet, weekly brief review, and a quarterly SEO audit. The feedback loop between content performance and content planning is 4-8 weeks. By the time an underperforming article is identified, 12 more articles have been commissioned in the same direction.

---

## Loom Script (~3 minutes)

"Let me walk you through Agentic Organic Growth OS — a multi-agent platform concept for B2B SaaS organic growth.

The organic growth problem I'm solving: content strategy, SEO execution, brand governance, and performance analysis are handled by different tools and different people with no unified operating loop. The result is slow feedback cycles, inconsistent brand voice, and content decisions made on 8-week-old data.

Agentic Organic Growth OS is a coordinated multi-agent platform that connects these functions: a content strategy agent that builds the editorial calendar from keyword, competitor, and topic signals; a brief generation agent that produces SEO-optimized, brand-aligned content briefs; a brand governance agent that checks drafts against brand voice guidelines before publication; a distribution agent that schedules and routes published content; and a performance intelligence agent that closes the feedback loop.

The key design insight: organic growth is a system, not a collection of individual tasks. The value comes from the agents operating together and sharing context — the performance agent's signals should directly inform the strategy agent's next editorial calendar.

I built a complete strategy for this: the agent architecture, the content workflow design, the brand governance system, and the SEO/GEO positioning intelligence framework."

## Key Screens

**Screen 1 — Agent Architecture Overview**
Highlight: 5 coordinated agents with defined handoffs. Each agent has a specific input, output, and approval gate. The coordination layer manages sequencing and context sharing between agents.

**Screen 2 — Brand Governance Design**
Highlight: Brand voice guidelines codified as evaluation criteria. The governance agent checks every draft before publication, scoring against tone, vocabulary, and positioning. Not a keyword filter — a nuanced voice consistency check.

**Screen 3 — GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) Strategy**
Highlight: Content designed for AI-powered answer engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Bing Chat) as well as traditional search. The strategy includes structured data, citation-friendly writing, and authority signal building — an emerging discipline in 2026.

## Interview Talking Points

**For "Tell me about a multi-agent system you designed":**
"The Agentic Growth OS is my clearest example of multi-agent coordination thinking. The challenge in multi-agent design is not building individual agents — it's designing the handoffs and shared context. I designed a coordination layer that passes structured context between agents: the performance agent's signals arrive at the strategy agent as structured topic recommendations, not raw analytics. Each agent does one thing well; the coordination layer makes them operate as a system."
